How It Works

From skin contact
to life-saving insight

Four invisible stages. One wearable. All the clinical data your doctor never had.

  • 01

    Sense

    Temperature, pressure, galvanic nerve response, and vibration sensors sample every 1–5 seconds. Silver-coated yarn doubles as both fabric and electrode.

  • 02

    Process

    An onboard MCU filters raw streams, converts via ADC, timestamps readings, and prepares clean data — all in a module smaller than a matchbox.

  • 03

    Analyse

    ML models detect early abnormalities across all biomarkers simultaneously, producing a risk score that no single sensor could achieve alone.

  • 04

    Act

    High-pressure zones trigger automatic pad inflation. High-risk readings push plain-language alerts to patient, caregiver, and clinician — instantly.

Common Questions

Answers to the hard ones

All electronics are sealed inside a detachable module that snaps out magnetically before washing. The textile — including the silver-coated sensing yarn — launders normally. Shieldex silver yarn retains its conductivity through repeated wash cycles, ensuring both hygiene and sensor performance.

The app presents intuitive color-coded risk indicators, not medical readouts. Caregivers and clinicians can monitor remotely and receive alerts independently — the patient doesn't need to manage the system at all. Large touch targets, minimal text, and family-account support make it accessible across all age groups.

Smart mats capture only a momentary snapshot when the patient stands on them. Tissue stress and circulation changes develop throughout the entire day. NeuroSole follows you everywhere, monitoring four distinct biological pathways simultaneously, giving clinicians a continuous picture a mat simply cannot.

We have a functional prototype with each sensor subsystem individually validated and real-time BLE data transmission demonstrated. Current focus is synchronized multi-sensor calibration toward CDSCO Class B (India) and FDA 510(k) (USA) regulatory pathways.

Patients purchase the electronic module once, while replacement textile socks are sold periodically as consumables — creating predictable recurring revenue. Institutional partnerships with hospitals and diabetic clinics enable bulk procurement via a hybrid B2C + B2B model.
